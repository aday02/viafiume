Where We Love to Eat

Our favorite spots for authentic Abruzzese cuisine

One of the greatest joys of living in Pescara is the incredible food scene. From family-run trattorias serving generations-old recipes to beachfront seafood spots and modern wine bars, we've discovered some real gems. Here are our regular haunts and special occasion favorites.

Dining Tips We've Learned

Meal Times

Lunch is typically 12:30-2:30pm, dinner starts around 7:30-8pm (or later in summer). Many restaurants close between lunch and dinner.

Reservations

For nicer restaurants, especially on weekends, book ahead. Many locals prefer to call rather than use online booking. We use Fork for convenient local restaurant reservations.

Coperto

Don't be surprised by the "coperto" (cover charge) of ‚Ç¨1-3 per person. This is standard in Italian restaurants and covers bread and table service.

Water

You'll be asked "naturale o frizzante?" (still or sparkling). Tap water is safe but not typically served in restaurants.

Coffee Culture

Cappuccino is a breakfast drink only! After meals, order an espresso. Standing at the bar is cheaper than table service.

Tipping

Service is usually included. Leaving a few euros for excellent service is appreciated but not expected. Round up for coffee at the bar.
